Project Title: Application of Mobile-Phone Technology to Advance Access to Sustainable PointOf-Use Water Purification Technologies for Household Drinking Water & Wastewater Reuse for Backyard Food Production (mWaF).

Access to clean and safe drinking water and nutritious food are key to uplifting public health, food security and sustainable development of the country.

Water borne diseases are reported to account for 4.1% of all global disease related deaths. In Uganda, Diarrhea alone is responsible for 22% of mortality of children under the age of five (5). Currently, water is predominantly boiled before drinking using biomass energy sources with wood being the commonest. This does not only cause upsurges in the carbon footprint through deforestation, but it is also expensive in-terms of energy costs for a population that is uncertain of attaining a food meal a day. Proven point-of-use (PoU) water purification/treatment technologies do not require boiling of water and are effective in mitigating health risks resulting from drinking unsafe water. Several novel and cost-effective PoU water treatment/purification technologies that have been tested are currently available on the Ugandan market. However, their use, accessibility and adoptability have been low

Backyard gardening is a recommended practice to boost household food and nutrition security. There are several urban gardening techniques currently available, although their dissemination has been slow.

Solutions are therefore needed to increase safe water availability in homes and communities, and food production for the ever-increasing urban population. This project aims at creating more distribution channels for existing and low-cost PoU water treatment technologies and urban gardening knowledge and services

Our objective is to create an inclusive water-food nexus that focuses on increasing household access to clean drinking water, and advance household gardening knowledge and water reuse to boost food security.

The specific objectives include:

  1. Developing a mobile-phone driven technology to create data driven distribution chains for existing proven PoU drinking water technologies and urban gardening techniques.
  2. Building capacity and disseminating PoU drinking water technologies and urban gardening knowledge and skills.
