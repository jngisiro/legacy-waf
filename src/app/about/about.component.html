About the PROJECT

Application of Mobile-Phone Technology to Advance Access to Sustainable Point-Of-Use

Water Purification Technologies for Household Drinking Water, Knowledge & Water Reuse for Backyard Food Production (mWaF project) under the supervision of Dr Prossie Nakawuka and Dr Joshua Wanyama.
The mWaF project targets the 19.8 million mobile-phone users in Uganda, of which 6 million are smartphone users and 46% (9.1 million people) are internet users8. Most of these are situated in urban areas;
with a population of about 7.4 million people; grappling with poor sanitation, hygiene, access to clean drinking water, waterborne diseases and food insecurity. The situation is worse in slum settlements
which are usually pigeonholed by poverty and inadequate access to water and thus are susceptible to sporadic outbreaks of waterborne diseases and hunger. In Kampala alone, 53.6% of the 1.5 million urban
population live under slum settings9. A slum is a densely populated urban informal settlement with inhabitants living in shoddy housing and low standards.
